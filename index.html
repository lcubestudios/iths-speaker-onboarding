About the Workshop

This workshop series intends to prepare ITHS students for a 2-day hackathon.
While students are expected to complete a project in this period—a website, app concept, or interactive digital experience—the greater goal is to expand their understanding of what computer science can offer, regardless of outcome.
This may include:

  • What's possible with today's tools and technology
  • What makes an idea viable
  • How real products are structured and brought to life
  • The skills, tools, and people required to build effectively
  • How to think, prioritize, and execute under constraints

Guidelines

  • Maintain a practical focus; avoid heavy theory
  • Slides are optional; the session should work as a standalone presentation. (A template has been provided below, though you may use your own materials or present without slides.)
  • Use real examples and experiences to support your points

Session Context

Audience

Suggested Structure

The following structure is provided as guidance and may be adapted as needed.

Minutes 0–10
Introduction
Who you are, what you do, how you arrived at your current role(s)
Minutes 10–25
Professional context
What matters in your field, common misconceptions, how real teams and products operate
Minutes 25–45
Practical demonstration
A product breakdown, a simple build, or a real workflow
Minutes 45–55
Hackathon application
What you would build within the given timeframe, what students should prioritize and avoid, what makes a project stand out
Minutes 55–60
Q&A
Open discussion
